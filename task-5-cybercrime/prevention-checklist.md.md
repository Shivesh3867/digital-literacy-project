# Stay Safe Online – Cyber Safety Checklist for College Students



# Financial & UPI Safety

Verify before you pay:

Always double-check the receiver’s name in your UPI app before sending money. Even small spelling differences can indicate fraud.

Never enter UPI PIN to receive money:

You only need your PIN while sending money. If someone asks for it to "receive" funds, it’s a scam.



# Account & Device Security

Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA):

Use apps like Google Authenticator for extra security on email, social media, and university accounts.

**Use strong and unique passwords:

Avoid using the same password across multiple platforms. A password manager can help.


# Academic & Career Safety

Be cautious of fake internships/jobs:

Avoid offers that promise high pay for simple work or ask for registration fees.

Always use official websites:

Check exam results, notices, or updates only from official university portals.



# Privacy & Social Media Safety

Avoid oversharing personal information:

Do not post ID cards, tickets, or live locations online. Share updates only after leaving the place.

Review app permissions regularly:

Remove access to contacts, camera, or files from apps you don’t fully trust.

---

# Reporting & Emergency Action

Report cyber fraud immediately:

If you are a victim, report it on the National Cyber Crime Portal: https://cybercrime.gov.in

Call helpline 1930:

For immediate financial fraud assistance, call 1930 as soon as possible to increase chances of recovery.
