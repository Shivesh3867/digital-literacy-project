# Case Study: The "Fake Buyer" UPI Payment Fraud

# What is UPI / Online Payment Fraud?

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a type of cybercrime where scammers trick users into sending money or approving fake payment requests. Instead of hacking systems directly, they manipulate people into authorizing transactions themselves using UPI apps like Google Pay, PhonePe, or Paytm.



# How It Typically Happens (Step-by-Step):

A student lists an item (like a phone, laptop, or notes) for sale on platforms such as OLX or WhatsApp groups.

A scammer contacts the student pretending to be a genuine buyer. They act very interested and try to build trust quickly.

The scammer says they will send an advance payment and then sends a "Collect Request" (payment request) through UPI.

The student receives a notification and assumes it is for receiving money, not sending it.

Without understanding, the student enters their UPI PIN to "accept" the request.

Instead of receiving money, the amount gets debited from the student’s account.

The scammer quickly blocks the victim and disappears.


# Who is Targeted?

Students, online sellers, and people new to digital payments are the most common targets. Anyone unfamiliar with how UPI requests work is especially vulnerable.



# The Consequences:

The victim suffers immediate financial loss, which can sometimes be a large amount.

It also leads to stress, panic, and loss of trust in online payment systems.

In many cases, recovering the money is difficult unless reported instantly to the bank or cybercrime portal.

Additionally, victims may feel embarrassed or hesitant to report the incident, which allows such scams to continue.



# Key Lesson:

Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money.  
UPI PIN is only required when sending money.

Always double-check payment requests and avoid trusting unknown buyers or urgent offers.
